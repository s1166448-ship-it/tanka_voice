Tanka Boatwoman
George Chinnery (1774–1852) | c. 1852 | Oil on canvas | 28 × 23.8 cm
George Chinnery painted numerous Tanka women, among whom A-lai and A-su were his most frequent models. The Tanka girls in his works are often portrayed with full figures, friendly smiles, and warm, approachable demeanors—qualities clearly present in this painting. This subject became one of his signature themes.

The woman in this painting wears a rattan hat and traditional Tanka clothing, holding a kerchief in her hand. Chinnery's signature style is evident throughout: swift, assured brushstrokes, and his beloved vermilion red used to highlight the softness of her cheeks, lips, arms, and fingers, creating delicate shadows. Through this elegant composition, a young woman of the working class is elevated into a vision of natural beauty.

✨ Chinnery brought this Tanka woman across the ocean, making her seen by the Western world.
